About
MOSTAFA MOSBAH
I was born on June 25th, 1995. Fun fact: I was born in a hospital two minutes away from the stadium of my favorite soccer club.
After living for five years in America, my family and I went back to my home country, Egypt, where I lived for a decade.
Soccer was a big part of my life growing up. I found solace every time I stepped on a soccer field.
In 2010, I moved to Queens, NY where I attended high school and university.
In 2014 I was accepted into Hunter College in NYC where I explored my interests in international politics and media.
In 2019, I graduated with a major in documentary filmmaking with a focus on film editing and a minor in sociology.
A few months after graduating, I moved to Los Angeles, CA to pursue a career in media production.
I held a retail job to support myself while doing freelance photography work after my shifts and over the weekends.
My goal was to land an entry-level job as a Production Assistant and get my foot in the door. That's why I started networking with everyone that I would run into.
March 19, 2020 was the day everyone's world turned upside down, including mine. I found myself out of a job and without a source of income. I knew something had to change.
I stumbled upon several coding bootcamp ads online and started looking into it. I spoke with a few friends in the field and decided to dedicate my time learning how to code.
After months of watching tutorials and researching different bootcamps, I decided to apply to Flatiron School. I was admitted and I start the Software Engineering Immersive program on June 28th, 2021.
